| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| High-risk recipients (consecutive exact-80-hr pattern) | **250** |
| Medium-risk recipients (single dominant provider) | **350** |
| Concurrent billing overlap flags — March 2026 | **14,720** |

| Consecutive exact-80-hour flags (strongest signal) | **1,000** |
| Dominant-provider flags across 6 months | **1,750** |

### Three Fraud Signal Types
1. **Exact 80-hr Consecutive Billing** — Recipient reports exactly 80 hours in two or more consecutive months. Probability of this occurring naturally across multiple months is extremely low. *250 high-risk cases identified.*
2. **Concurrent Billing Overlaps** — Activity timestamps overlap across different providers within the same time window, suggesting possible double-billing. *17.1% flag rate in March.*
3. **Dominant Provider Concentration** — One provider accounts for ≥80% of a recipient's hours over consecutive months, an indicator of potentially coached or coordinated reporting. *350 medium-risk cases.*

> *"We've flagged 600 recipients across two risk tiers — that's 6% of the enrolled population showing at least one suspicious billing pattern. The concurrent overlap rate of 17% warrants further investigation."*

## Key Narrative Arc

1. **Growing program** — activity volume grew month-over-month, with March 2026 being the peak at 86K records and 45 avg hours
2. **Compliance gap** — only 1 in 10 recipients is hitting the 80-hour monthly target; the program has significant room to improve
3. **State inequity** — a 35-point gap between top and bottom states signals that program support and outreach is not evenly distributed
4. **Fraud signals present** — 6% of enrollees show at least one suspicious pattern; concurrent billing at 17% is the widest-impact flag
5. **Demographic opportunity** — the 65+ cohort (20%) likely needs tailored interventions to reach the hours threshold given mobility and access constraints
